What is MFA and Why It Matters for Your Business

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) adds an extra layer of security to your accounts.

MFA requires users to provide multiple forms of verification, making it harder for attackers to access accounts:

  1. Password (something you know).
  2. Phone or app code (something you have).
  3. Biometric verification (something you are, optional).

Enabling MFA in Microsoft 365 significantly reduces phishing and account compromise risks.
